General

All results are based on a sample of about XXXX Flemish individuals from the EU Survey on Income and Living Conditions (EU-SILC). The results of the total Flemish population are expected to go in the same direction, but are susceptible to some differences due to under-representation of some individuals at the upper or lower tail of the distribution. Furthermore, the EU-SILC survey contains incomplete fiscal information (for instance, municipal taxes, but also many deductible expenses , are not included in the model).

Since Mefisto is an arithmetic model, no behavioral reactions are included. Please be aware that agents in the model do not change their economic behavior such as their labor supply, their expenditure behaviour or demographic status (being married or not, having children or not) due to the implemented policy changes. If available, detailed analyses of policy changes including behavioral reactions can be found on www.flemosi.be

The impact of the policy changes is measured on the basis of individual disposable income. The household composition is taken into account in the standard way, which is by using OECD equivalence scales. Equivalized disposable incomes are divided equally among all members of the household, ignoring within household inequality.

Gains and losses

Individuals are classified in deciles according to their disposable equivalized income before the reform. The average impact of the reform for a given decile is presented in Euros per month. A more detailed analysis according to other classifications can be obtained by clicking on 'more'.

Budget Breakdown

The budgetary impacts are presented in millions of Euros per year. A more detailed breakdown according to deciles can be obtained by clicking on 'more'.

Poverty

The poverty line for a single is fixed at 60% of the median, which is about 960 euro a month. The poverty rate measures the percentage of Flemish individuals below this threshold. The total poverty gap measures the total amount of money necessary to bring all individuals in poverty up to the poverty line. The poverty gap per poor person divides this number by the total number of poor individuals. A more detailed breakdown according to deciles can be obtained by clicking on 'more'.

Inequality

The Gini coefficient is a measure of the inequality of a distribution, a value of 0 expressing total equality and a value of 1 maximal inequality. The Lorenz curve is a graphical representation of the distribution of the equivalized disposable incomes; it shows for the bottom percentage of individuals, how much of total income accrues to them (also in percentage).
